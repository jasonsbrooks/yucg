Strategy Consulting

We have clientele ranging from multinational corporations to fledgling startups. Our status as an undergraduate organization gives us a fresh perspective on twenty-first century market problems. Strategy Consulting members are passionate about applying creative solutions to challenging issues, expediting the flow of information from native sources to clients, and using every opportunity to improve their breadth of knowledge.

Members of Strategy Consulting have gone on to internships and full-time positions with McKinsey and Co., the Boston Consulting Group, The Parthenon Group, and other major consulting firms. In fact, the interview process for admittance to this division requires a case interview. Members also practice case interviews as a group to develop a professional approach to problem solving and prepare for the job market after Yale.

Marketing Consulting

The Marketing Consulting branch applies fresh thinking and up-to-date methodologies to develop effective marketing strategies for our clients. For scalable, high-impact, and low-cost marketing campaigns we emphasize use of online portals and networks. As college students with dispodable incomes, we offer an unparalleled opportunity to take advantage of the latest trends relevant to Generation Y customers. We also conduct marketing campaigns themselves, adverising services and products to students from the source they find most reliable - their peers.

Product Development

The Product Development Group specializes in assisting clients in a wide array of programming oriented spaces, including Web 2.0 apps, mobile apps, big data analysis, and algorithm development. Product Development Group recruits heavily from the Yale Computer Science department. Members have received full-time and internship offers from large technology firms such as Google, Oracle, and Microsoft, as well as smaller companies like Pixorial, Fog Creek, and TripAdvisor. In fact, application to the Product Development Group requires a technical interview with questions often used for development opportunities with the aforementioned firms.

Teams in this division also include business oriented members who would otherwise join a Strategy Consulting Team. These members assess our clients' business models and product feature sets to ensure their programmer partners devote their resources to the creating technology well suited for problem at hand.

About Us

Who We Are

The Yale Undergraduate Consulting Group (formerly Maya) is an undergraduate-run consulting group offering strategy consulting, marketing, and product development services to businesses. We offer a full range of consulting services, take a hands-on approach to developing applicable strategies, and help implement them for our clients.

Teams of four or five students are led by a Project Manager who acts as the primary client liaison and ensures the team provides only the highest quality outcome. Teams are designed to include a balance of experienced upperclassmen and newcomers, and they often include students with internship experience at major consulting, financial, marketing, or product develoment firms. The recruitment process is a careful, highly selective process modeled after the best practices of management consulting and technology firms so our clients can be confident that only the most dedicated and effective students are on each engagement.

Campusfood.com allows millions of students across the country to have easy access to a network of over 2500 restaurant. Founded by President Michael Saunders in 1997, who was at that time a student at the University of Pennsylvannia, the organization now serves over 300 campuses. The virtual food court allows students to place orders from their favorite local restaurants conveniently, making the hassle of ordering food over the phone a thing of the past.

Campusfood.com came to YUCG in order to use our unique student perspective to expand into the Yale market. To start, we conducted market research in order to effectively create a marketing campaign for Campusfood.com. YUCG then lead the promotional efforts at Yale, predominately focusing the campaign through social networking sites and on-campus student events.
HavetoHave.com (previously called wingtipit.com) is a site where users can create individual lists of clothing and other products they see online so they can keep them bookmarked for themselves or share them with friends. Users download a toolbar icon that they can click on when they are shopping online and want to add an item to their list. HavetoHave receives a commission on purchases bought through use of the site.
The Hotlist is the first geo-social aggregator to tell users what’s going on today, tomorrow and throughout the week in their area and personal network. The Hotlist brings together location-specific data from sites like Facebook, Twitter, Google, Yelp and others to calculate the popularity of specific events and display useful details like the guy to girl ratio and reviews. Unique to The Hotlist is the combined information on places, events, friends and friends of friends that offers the most complete – and customized – picture of what’s happening.


Proximiant allows Android and Apple smartphone users to receive digital receipts. The phone application is a simple, convenient online tool to organize retail purchases.

YUCG provided an analysis of the market potential of digital receipts and the adoption of Proximiant’s services, specifically in the New Haven region. Research and data analysis conducted include qualitative measures through online research and competitive analysis.


Jojo’s Coffee and Tea is an independently owned coffee chain based in Connecticut that specializes in high-quality coffee, tea, smoothies and baked goods.

Jojo’s wanted to build name recognition in the Yale community as it views its distance from Yale’s campus as an impediment to students enjoying its superior quality coffee and tea. Jojo’s was also developing a website to attract people to their retail locations. It sought YUCG’s help in both the design and execution of viral campaigns to promote its store and website (currently down for a redesign). YUCG also organized a widely attended open-mic night in their New Haven branch to familiarize Yale students with its location.


PointWC sought YUCG's services to evaluate the possibility of expanding their French brand to the American market. In order to guide their decision, YUCG conducted extensive market research to determine the current state of the luxury bathroom market as well as profile the immediate competition. To assess consumer demand for the client's product and services, YUCG conducted surveys in several US cities and analysed the results. Ultimately, this research was synthesized and combined into a formal report recommending what steps PointWC should take to expand to the USA.


Teach For America (TFA) is the national corps of recent college graduates of all academic majors and career interests who commit two years to teach in urban and rural public schools seeking to expand educational opportunity.

To help with recuitment efforts on the Yale campus, YUCG executed an online marketing scheme to email appropriate panlists to publicize deadlines and created a Facebook event for submission deadlines and friend invitations.


The Yale Bookstore provides Yale-specific merchandise at its location on 77 Broadway as well as on the Yale Insignia website. The bookstore updates its products and provides promotional discounts to Yale students throughout the year to adjust to shifting consumer preferences.

YUCG provided consulting and marketing guidance to the Bookstore on its choices of merchandise and times to offer promotions as well as advertise the promotions themselves. We provided consulting aid which included surveys and focus groups to assess demand for merchandise relating to residential colleges, women, insignia, ebooks, and rental textbooks.


ZS Associates is a consulting firm specializing in sales and marketing that recruits Yale undergraduates. They interpret quantitative data to develop effective strategies for companies to increase demand.

YUCG was hired to do on campus promotion to help ZS Associates to attract more job applicants. We hosted an info session for the company on campus, created, and distributed fliers to advertise the event and the application information.


Circle of Women researches policy solutions to bring educational opportunities to girls in the developing world. Past solutions included rebuilding a school for girls that was destroyed by the Taliban in Afghanistan, building technology rooms and toilets for girls in Pakistan, and constructing a sustainable roof in Rhema, India. Each solution has been driven by an individual chapter.

The Yale Chapter of Circle of Women constructed a project that could be implemented at every Circle school. The project constructed a financial literacy campaign using multimedia technology to high school girls to connect the dots between microfinance and education. Since the project is of a differing structure and of a larger scale, it needed YUCG to create a distinct brand.
